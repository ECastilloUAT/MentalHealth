What is Mental Health

Mental Health: a personâ€™s condition with regard to their psychological and emotional well-being.

It affects how we think, feel, and act, and plays a crucial role in our overall well-being. Mental health is not just the absence of mental illness, but also involves how we manage stress, relate to others, and make decisions. Just like physical health, mental health is an ongoing process that can fluctuate over time.

Mental Health Conditions

Anxiety Disorders

Conditions like Generalized Anxiety Disorder, Panic Disorder, and Social Anxiety Disorder, which involve excessive worry and fear.



Mood Disorders

These include Depression and Bipolar Disorder, which affect emotions, moods, and feelings of self-worth.



Eating Disorders

Such as Anorexia, Bulimia, and Binge Eating Disorder, which involve unhealthy eating habits and a distorted body image.



Psychotic Disorders

Conditions like Schizophrenia, which may cause distorted thinking, hallucinations, or delusions.



Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

A condition involving repetitive, intrusive thoughts and compulsive behaviors.



Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

A reaction to experiencing or witnessing traumatic events.



Personality Disorders

Including Borderline Personality Disorder, which affects emotional regulation and interpersonal relationships.



Neurodevelopmental Disorders

Such as Autism Spectrum Disorder, which impacts behavior, communication, and social interaction.

Signs and Symptoms of Mental Health Issues

Emotional changes

like feelings of sadness, anxiety, or irritability.

Physical symptoms

such as fatigue, changes in appetite or sleep patterns.

Behavioral changes

including withdrawal from activities or people, or increased use of substances.

Persistent thoughts

of self-harm, hopelessness, or worthlessness.



Risk Factors and Causes

Genetic and Biological

Family history of mental illness can increase the risk of developing similar conditions.

Environmental Influences

Traumatic experiences, childhood abuse, or exposure to violence can contribute.

Lifestyle Factors

Substance abuse, lack of physical activity, and poor nutrition can impact mental health.

Socioeconomic Factors

Financial difficulties, discrimination, and social inequality can increase stress.

Social Isolation

A lack of social support or relationships can worsen mental health.
